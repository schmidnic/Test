    The AI landscape

    Four slides on the organizations and model types that define the current AI ecosystem.

    Slide 1 of 4

    Five organizations that matter most

    Most of the AI models you interact with today come from five organizations: OpenAI (GPT series), Anthropic (Claude), Google DeepMind (Gemini), Meta AI (Llama), and Mistral AI. Each approaches AI development differently: different training methods, different safety philosophies, different openness to external access. Knowing who builds what helps you evaluate the tools and understand why they behave differently.

    Slide 2 of 4

    Closed models: access via API

    Closed models (GPT, Claude, Gemini) run on the provider's infrastructure. You interact with them through an API or consumer product. You cannot download the weights or run them locally. The provider controls updates, pricing, and availability. This model maximizes ease of use but creates dependency. Changes made by the provider, including safety filtering changes, apply to everyone using the API.

    Slide 3 of 4

    Open source models: running locally

    Open source models (Llama, Mistral, Falcon, and others) release their weights publicly. Anyone can download and run them on their own hardware. This enables full control: you can fine-tune, inspect, deploy offline, and avoid per-token fees. The tradeoff is infrastructure cost and expertise. Running a competitive open source model requires significant hardware. Meta's release of Llama changed the economics of AI development significantly.

    Slide 4 of 4

    Benchmarks and what they actually measure

    AI benchmarks measure model performance on standardized tests: reasoning, coding, math, language understanding. They provide a shared vocabulary for comparing models. But benchmarks can be gamed: models are sometimes fine-tuned on benchmark data, inflating scores. Performance on benchmarks does not always correlate with performance on real tasks. Treat benchmarks as directional signals, not definitive rankings.

    Model families

    Four concepts for navigating the AI landscape. Click each to expand.

    A foundation model is a large base model trained on broad data, capable of many tasks. GPT-4, Claude 3, and Gemini 1.5 are foundation models. They serve as starting points for more specialized applications through fine-tuning. The term emphasizes that these models are not finished products but foundations. Most specialized AI applications in enterprise settings are fine-tuned versions of foundation models, not models trained from scratch.

    A model family is a set of models from the same provider sharing the same architecture and training approach but differing in size and capability. OpenAI's GPT-4 family includes GPT-4o, GPT-4o mini, and o1. Anthropic's Claude 3 family includes Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus. Larger models in a family are more capable but slower and more expensive. Understanding model families lets you choose the right size for a task, balancing cost, latency, and capability.

    Different organizations approach AI safety differently. Anthropic was founded specifically around safety research and developed techniques like Constitutional AI and RLHF-based alignment. OpenAI has a safety team and publishes safety research. Meta prioritizes openness, arguing that wider access enables better collective safety research. Google DeepMind has a dedicated safety team alongside its capabilities research. These different approaches produce models with different default behaviors, refusal patterns, and willingness to discuss sensitive topics.

    Multimodal models can process more than one type of input: text plus images, audio, video, or code. GPT-4o, Claude 3, and Gemini are all multimodal. This changes what these systems can do significantly: reading and describing images, transcribing audio, analyzing diagrams, understanding screenshots. Multimodality is now expected in frontier models. The modalities available and their quality differ across providers.

    OpenAI

    Founded in 2015, first as nonprofit, later capped-profit. Creator of the GPT series and the o1/o3 reasoning models. ChatGPT, launched in November 2022, was the product that brought large language models to mainstream attention. Largest model family in terms of enterprise adoption. Close partnership with Microsoft, which provides compute and integrates models into its products.

    Anthropic

    Founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers including Dario and Daniela Amodei. Safety-focused by founding mission. Developed Constitutional AI and the Claude model family. Backed by Google and Amazon. Known for longer context windows and strong instruction-following. Claude models are widely used in enterprise and developer contexts.

    Meta AI

    Meta releases its Llama models with open weights, a significant decision that changed the open source AI landscape. Llama 3 and 3.1 are competitive with closed models on many benchmarks. The open release means anyone can fine-tune, inspect, and deploy Llama locally. Meta's argument: open AI is safer AI because more eyes can find problems.

    Mistral AI

    French AI startup founded in 2023 by former Google DeepMind and Meta researchers. Punches above its weight: smaller models (7B parameters) that perform comparably to much larger models. Fully open source. Significant in European AI policy discussions. Le Chat is their consumer product. Relevant for anyone building EU-based AI systems with data sovereignty requirements.

    Open vs closed

    What is the key practical difference between open source and closed models?

    Weights vs. API

    Closed models: you access them through an API, run on the provider's servers, pay per token, cannot inspect or modify the weights. Open source models: you download the weights and run them yourself. Full control, no per-token cost at inference time, but requires infrastructure. Both approaches have legitimate use cases.

    Foundation

    Why are they called 'foundation models'?

    A base, not a finished product

    Foundation models are large base models trained on broad data. They are the starting point for more specialized applications through fine-tuning. The term emphasizes that these are not complete solutions but foundations. Most enterprise AI applications are fine-tuned foundation models, not models trained from scratch.

    Model size

    What does it mean when a model has '7 billion parameters'?

    Numerical weights

    Parameters are the numerical weights that define the model's behavior, adjusted during training. 7 billion parameters means 7 billion numbers. Larger parameter counts generally enable more capability but require more compute to run. A 7B model can run on a high-end laptop. A 70B model requires a workstation. A 700B model requires a server cluster.

    Multimodal

    What does it mean for a model to be multimodal?

    Multiple input types

    A multimodal model can process more than text: images, audio, video, documents, or code. GPT-4o can describe images and transcribe audio. Claude 3 can read and analyze images. Gemini was designed multimodal from the start. Multimodality is now standard in frontier models and significantly expands what these systems can be used for.

    What to take with you

    The AI landscape is not one model and one company. It is a competitive ecosystem with meaningfully different approaches to openness, safety, capability, and deployment. Knowing the main players and their flagship models gives you a map, not just data points.

    Open source and closed source are not better or worse in absolute terms. They represent different tradeoffs: control vs. ease of use, infrastructure cost vs. per-token cost, inspectability vs. convenience. The right choice depends on use case, data privacy requirements, and available engineering resources.

    Benchmarks are useful but limited. They measure performance on standardized tasks, which may not reflect performance on your specific task. A model that ranks second on a benchmark may be first for your use case. Always evaluate on your own data when making consequential decisions.
